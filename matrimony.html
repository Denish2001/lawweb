KENYA’S SUPREME COURT DECISION ON DISTRIBUTION OF MATRIMONIAL PROPERTY

JOSEPH OMBOGI OGENTOTO V MARTHA BOSIBORI OGENTOTO PETITION NO. 11 OF 2020

This was a matrimonial cause that was instituted in the High Court by a divorced wife, the respondent herein. The Respondent wanted properties acquired during the substance of the marriage be shared equally save for one that she wanted wholly. The High Court in its finding distinguished matrimonial property from other individually acquired properties. It noted further that marriage is not a passport to have property acquired your spouse thus the court awarded the wife 30% of the matrimonial home and 20% of the rental units within the same compound for reasons that she had not proven direct contribution but contributed indirectly to the acquisition of the matrimonial property.

On appeal to the Court of Appeal the court set aside the High Courts finding and argued that the Respondent had adduced enough evidence to show direct contribution that the court didn’t take into account and ordered that matrimonial property and rental units built thereon be shared 50:50. Aggrieved by the decision the divorced husband appealed to the Supreme Court. The supreme court held as follows:

  1. Article 45(3) does not imply 50:50 in distribution but equitable distribution of matrimonial property. Court in determining distribution of matrimonial property will take into consideration the amount contribution on acquisition by each party.
  2. Marriage is not a passport to automatically acquire 50% of the matrimonial property.
  3. Distribution shall be done on a case by case basis.
The appeal at Supreme court was dismissed for reason that the Respondent had contributed monetarily and indirectly thus deserving the 50%.
